Use of Active and Passive Cooling Systems in Conjunction to Enhance Cooling Efficiency in Electric Circuits

Writer: Akash Desai

There are two major means of cooling electronic circuitries, passive and active cooling. Passive cooling builds on the principle of conduction and convection of heat through its surrounding mediums via contact (such as a heat sink). Thus, this uses no external energy for heat dissipation. Active cooling, however, relies on an external power source for pumping the heat (such as a fans or liquid cooling systems).
