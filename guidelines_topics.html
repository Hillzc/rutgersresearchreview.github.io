Guidelines: Topics


If you are a Rutgers undergraduate student, you are eligible to submit an article that centers on an emerging topic, concept, or innovative research in any discipline. These articles serve to explore a particular idea. There is no particular structure for Topics articles. You should be able to properly introduce the topic at hand and flow logically and coherently.

At the end of the article, list your references in APA format in alphabetical order. Please ensure that the article contains ~700 words. Figures and/or tables with proper descriptions are strongly encouraged.

    Questions to address (in no particular order):

  • What is your topic of focus? Make sure to expand on the current research in this area.
  • What specific research or discovery (include citations) in this area interests you?
  • What is the significance of this research and why is it important to you?
  • Why should the audience care? In other words, how will the general populace be affected in the near or distant future?
  • Is there any overlap between this research and another?
  • What do you think is the future direction of this research?

Submit your article to rutgersresearchreview@gmail.com. Your article will be reviewed by a Rutgers Research Review editor who is familiar with your general field of research. The comments will be sent to you for revision. After you submit the second draft with the recommended changes to the editor, the editor will make any remaining edits and publish the article on the Rutgers Research Review website.
